The Sentiment Polarity Category classifies how positively or negatively people talk of tweets about rivers on Twitter. Each tweet is assigned a score, which can have a between -5 (extremely negative) and 5 (extremely positive) but will usually fall somewhere in between.

Tweets are aggreagtaed at each geographical scale (River Basin District, Management Catchment, Operational Catchment and Waterbody) and classified into three categories; tweets with a polarity score in the lower third ("Negative Sentiment Engagement") is a score between -3.336 to -.0.59 and are coloured red; tweets with a polarity score in the middle third ("Neutral Sentiment Engagement") is a score between -0.059 and 0.303 and are coloured yellow; tweets with a polarity score in the upper third ("Positive Sentiment Engagement") is a score between 0.303 and 7.857 and are coloured green. Each category has roughly the same number of tweets sentiment is coloured green.
